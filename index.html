Mission of chrisland

Chrisland University's mission is to cultivate a moral and intellectual culture, attract global faculty, and educate holistically.

Vision

Chrisland University aspires to be a world-class institution known for intellectual freedom, ethics, research, and leadership training.

Motto

Intellectual Radiance: University motto emphasizing excellence, ethics, character, and community service in education

Philosophy

Chrisland University prioritizes character and learning, maintaining zero tolerance for indiscipline, drug abuse, cultism, and truancy.

Services

This are the roles performed by the office of the Vice-Chancellor

Administrative Leadership: The Vice Chancellor provides overall administrative leadership and direction to the university, ensuring that academic and administrative policies are implemented effectively.

Academic Planning and Development: The Vice Chancellor oversees the development and implementation of academic programs, curriculum, and research initiatives to enhance the quality of education offered by the university.

Financial Management: The Vice Chancellor is responsible for managing the university's budget and finances, including allocating funds for various academic and administrative activities.

Faculty and Staff Management: The Vice Chancellor supervises faculty recruitment, promotion, and professional development, as well as the hiring and management of administrative staff.

External Relations: The Vice Chancellor represents the university in interactions with government agencies, industry partners, donors, and the community, fostering collaborations and partnerships to support the university's mission and goals.

Strategic Planning: The Vice Chancellor leads the development and implementation of the university's strategic plan, setting long-term goals and objectives to guide its growth and development.

Quality Assurance: The Vice Chancellor ensures that the university maintains high academic standards and quality assurance processes, including accreditation and assessment activities.

The Vice Chancellor - Professor (Mrs.) Chinedum Peace Babalola

(B.Pharm, PhD, FAS, FAAS, FPSN, FPCPharm, FNAPharm, MIPAN)

Professor Chinedum Peace Babalola, a distinguished Pharmacist and former Dean of the Faculty of Pharmacy at the University of Ibadan, has made significant contributions to research and academia. She holds degrees from the University of Ife and the University of British Columbia, and has conducted extensive research in pharmacokinetics, pharmacodynamics, and pharmacogenetics, focusing on optimizing drug therapy for Nigerians. Throughout her career, Prof. Babalola has held various leadership roles, including Head of Department and Director of the General Studies Program at the University of Ibadan. She has received numerous awards and grants for her research, and has established initiatives such as the Centre for Drug Discovery Development and Production. Prof. Babalola is actively involved in public health initiatives and community service, and is a member of several international and national organizations. She has mentored numerous students and junior colleagues, and has published extensively in reputable journals. Outside of academia, Prof. Babalola is dedicated to her faith and family. She assists her husband in church ministry and oversees women's and youth ministries. She is married to Venerable Collins Olufemi Babalola, and they have three children. Overall, Prof. Babalola's career is characterized by her commitment to excellence in research, education, and service to society.

The Deputy vice chancellor - Professor Ebenezer Olatunde Farombi

Professor Ebenezer Olatunde Farombi is a distinguished figure in the field of Biochemistry, boasting a rich career spanning over 34 years in academia, research, and administrative roles at the university level. He holds esteemed fellowships from institutions including the Royal Society of Chemistry and the Nigerian Academy of Science, underscoring his eminence in the field. His academic journey includes earning degrees from the University of Ibadan and specialized training in environmental health and biomedical research, highlighting his commitment to continuous learning and professional development. Professor Farombi's research endeavors have primarily focused on Molecular Toxicology, Cellular Oxidative Stress Mechanisms, and Nutrigenomics. His work is particularly notable for elucidating the chemopreventive properties of phytochemicals and their potential in combating neurodegenerative diseases such as Parkinson's. His contributions have garnered international recognition, with rankings among the top scientists globally and the receipt of numerous grants and fellowships. Beyond his research pursuits, Professor Farombi is esteemed as a mentor, having supervised hundreds of students and contributed to over 300 scientific articles in reputable international journals. His dedication to advancing the fields of biochemistry and toxicology underscores his profound impact on scientific understanding and societal well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to our Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) section, where we address some common queries about our university.

  • What are the admission requirements for undergraduate/graduate programs?

    Admission requirements for undergraduate/graduate programs vary depending on the specific program and department. You can find detailed information about admission requirements on our university website or by contacting the admissions office directly.

  • How does the university address issues of campus safety and security?

    Our university prioritizes the safety and security of all students, faculty, and staff. We have a dedicated campus security team that patrols the campus 24/7, and we have implemented various safety measures, such as CCTV surveillance and other safety systems

  • What opportunities are there for student involvement in extracurricular activities and clubs?

    We offer a wide range of extracurricular activities and clubs to cater to diverse interests and passions. Students can participate in academic clubs, sports teams, volunteer groups, and more. Our Student Affairs office actively supports student-led initiatives and provides resources for students to start their own clubs with the permission of the school.

  • What measures are in place to promote academic integrity and prevent plagiarism?

    Academic integrity is of utmost importance at our university, and we have strict policies and measures in place to promote ethical behavior and prevent plagiarism. We provide comprehensive training on academic integrity to all students and faculty members, and we use plagiarism detection software to screen all academic submissions for originality. Additionally, our faculty members are vigilant in monitoring student work and addressing any instances of academic dishonesty promptly and fairly.

  • How does the university handle complaints or grievances from students?

    Our university has established procedures for handling complaints and grievances from students in a fair and transparent manner. Students are encouraged to first attempt to resolve issues informally with the relevant faculty or staff member. If the issue remains unresolved, students can file a formal complaint through our designated channels, such as the Dean of Students office or the Office of Student Affairs. All complaints are thoroughly investigated, and appropriate actions are taken to address the concerns raised by students.
